The passionate historian of the Second World War

Olivier Wieviorka, historian emeritus and prolific writer, is a leading figure in French historical research. His brilliant career, punctuated by numerous publications, reflects a deep passion for the Second World War and an exceptional academic record.

Olivier Wieviorka

Youth and studies

Olivier Wieviorka was born in 1960 in Paris, France. His fascination with history began in his youth, while studying at the Lycée Janson de Sailly. He went on to pursue his higher education at the Université Paris I Panthéon-Sorbonne, where he obtained a master's degree in history. His quest for knowledge led him to earn a PhD in contemporary history, an essential milestone in his academic training.

Passion for the Second World War:

World War II has always been a major source of inspiration for Olivier Wieviorka. His interest in this tumultuous period of history turned into a real passion. He has devoted decades to studying in depth the political, social and military aspects of the war, while at the same time focusing on analyzing the lasting consequences of this event on contemporary society.

His works

Olivier Wieviorka is the author of many major works that have left their mark on historical research. Among his most notable works are "Oran, July 5, 1962: Un massacre oublié," in which he examines the tragic events of the Algerian War, as well as "La France à la Libération," an in-depth exploration of France during the Liberation period. His expertise and talent as a writer have been recognized by his peers, making him an undisputed authority on contemporary history. He has also contributed to numerous collective works and actively participated in interdisciplinary research projects. In conclusion, Olivier Wieviorka embodies the commitment and passion of a historian devoted to the study of the Second World War. His impressive academic career and seminal works make him a key player on the French historical scene, sharing with the public his in-depth understanding of this crucial period in world history.

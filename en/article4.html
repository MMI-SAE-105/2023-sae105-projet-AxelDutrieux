Memory of the 7th Cie

An international history

Olivier Wieviorka's 2023 book offers a transnational and comparative approach to resistance in Western Europe during the Second World War. It draws on British, American and European archives to analyze the interactions between clandestine forces, governments in exile and the Allies.

Cover of the book Une histoire de la résistance en Europe occidentale

A historian and author of numerous works

Olivier Wieviorka is a French historian and academic, specializing in the history of the Second World War. He is a professor at the École normale supérieure Paris-Saclay and a member of the Institut des sciences sociales du politique. He is the author of numerous reference works on the Resistance, the Normandy landings, the memory of the dark years and the total history of the global conflict.

Providing a transnational vision of resistance

Olivier Wieviorka's book aims to go beyond national and mythical visions of resistance to offer a global and nuanced history of the Shadow War in Europe. It examines six countries occupied by Nazi Germany: Norway, Denmark, the Netherlands, Belgium, France and Italy. It shows how resistance was organized, with what difficulties and successes, in a variety of political, social and cultural contexts. It highlights the essential role played by the British (SOE) and American (OSS) secret services in providing logistical, financial and strategic support to the underground forces. It traces the complex relationships between resistance fighters, governments in exile and the Allies, between cooperation and tension. It assesses the impact of the Resistance on the course of the war and on the liberation and reconstruction processes of European countries.

Many historical references

Olivier Wieviorka's book is based on numerous historical facts such as Churchill's creation of the SOE in July 1940 to "set Europe ablaze" against Nazi Germany, the United States' entry into the war in December 1941 and the establishment of the OSS in February 1942 to coordinate special operations, the United States' entry into the war in December 1941 and the establishment of the OSS in February 1942 to coordinate special operations, Italy's switch to the Allied camp in September 1943 and the formation of a National Liberation Committee bringing together the main political parties, The Allied landings in Normandy on June 6, 1944 and the role of the French Resistance's intelligence and sabotage networks, The general strike launched by the Belgian National Council of the Resistance in August-September 1944 to support the Allied advance, and The national uprising in the Netherlands in April-May 1945.

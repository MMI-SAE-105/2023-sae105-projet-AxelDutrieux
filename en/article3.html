Memory of the 7th Cie

A melody of resistance and emotion in cinema

Music, throughout history, has served as a powerful vector of emotion and expression. One such melody that has transcended eras and generations is the Chant des Partisans. This emblematic song, created in the midst of the Second World War, has profoundly marked history and found a significant place in the world of cinema.

Laissez passer of the Free French Forces by Anna Marlyn

Three people behind the song

The Chant des Partisans, also known as the Chant de la Libération, came into being during the dark years of the German Occupation in France. The French Resistance was looking for an anthem to unite the combatants and bolster the nation's morale. Anna Marly, a Russian-born singer-songwriter committed to the Resistance, sought to provide musical support for the anonymous heroes fighting for freedom. She was the creator of the melody that appealed to Joseph Kessel. He then asked Maurice Druon to write a new lyric. Involved in the Resistance herself, she sought to provide musical support for the anonymous heroes who were fighting for freedom.

The song of resistance at the service of La 7ème compagnie

The "Chant des Partisans" and the French comedy La 7ème Compagnie are each symbols of French culture, but they come from very different contexts. Le Chant des Partisans is a song of the French Resistance during the Second World War. It is associated with the struggle of French resistance fighters against German occupation. Whereas La 7ème Compagnie is a series of French comedy films, created by Robert Lamoureux, set during the Second World War. The link between Le Chant des Partisans and La 7ème Compagnie is quite simple: the song is used ironically in the film series to create a comic contrast. In Mais où est donc passé la 7ème Compagnie? the song is used during a scene in which the main characters are in trouble, trying to hide from the Germans. The contrast between the gravity of the situation in reality and the humorous use of the song creates a memorable comic effect.

More than a song, a hope

Le Chant des Partisans has become much more than just a song. During the war, it was the anthem of the Resistance, a rallying cry for the fighters in the shadows. It played a crucial role in motivating and unifying the various Resistance groups, transcending their ideological differences. The song recalls the determination of the French people to fight for freedom and resist oppression. After the war, the Chant des Partisans became a symbol of French memory of the Second World War. It recalls the courage of the Resistance fighters and their heroic struggle for freedom. Used in commemorations and historical events, it continues to celebrate the indomitable spirit of the French people. In short, the Chant des Partisans is much more than just a melody; it's a timeless anthem of resistance, hope and determination. Its use in film, notably in L'Armée des Ombres, reminds the world of the power of music to convey emotion and celebrate the courage of the men and women who fought for freedom during the darkest hours of history.
